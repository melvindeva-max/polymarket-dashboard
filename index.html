Position Sizing: Kelly Criterion

The Kelly Criterion helps determine optimal bet size: f* = (bp - q) / b, where f is the fraction to bet, b is the odds, p is win probability, and q is loss probability (1-p).
  • Never bet more than the Kelly fraction to reduce volatility
  • Half-Kelly is conservative and recommended for most traders
  • Cap position size at 5% of total bankroll
  • Diversify across multiple trades to reduce drawdown risk

Risk Management

  • Never risk more than 5% of total bankroll on a single position
  • Diversify across different categories (politics, sports, finance, etc.)
  • Set clear exit prices before entering a trade
  • Take profits at 2-3x initial risk for asymmetric payoffs
  • Avoid concentration in low-liquidity markets

Bankroll Management

  • Never bet more than you can afford to lose completely
  • Treat prediction markets as speculation, not investment
  • Use stop-loss orders to limit downside on macro bets
  • Keep at least 3 months of living expenses in cash reserves
  • Regularly review your trading record for edge validation
