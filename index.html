Kelly Criterion (Simplified)

Optimal bet size = (edge / odds). For a market at 60¬¢ where you believe true probability is 75%: edge = 0.75 ‚àí 0.60 = 0.15, odds = 0.40 (what you lose). Kelly = 0.15/0.40 = 37.5% of bankroll. In practice, use ¬º Kelly (‚âà9%) to reduce variance.

The 5% Rule

Never allocate more than 5% of your total portfolio to a single speculative position (Risk 3). For medium-confidence plays (Risk 2), cap at 15%. Only high-confidence plays (Risk 1) should exceed 15%, and even then cap at 25%.

Risk Management Tips

Diversification

Spread across uncorrelated categories ‚Äî politics, crypto, AI, sports, culture. A crypto crash won't affect election outcomes. Aim for 5‚Äì10 active positions across at least 3 categories.

Liquidity Check

Before entering, check the order book depth. Markets with less than $5K liquidity can have wide spreads (5%+). You'll lose edge just entering and exiting. Prefer markets with $50K+ liquidity.

Time Decay

Markets near resolution date converge to 0 or 100. If you're holding a YES position at 90¬¢ with 2 weeks left, your upside is 10¬¢ but downside is 90¬¢. Consider taking profits early when your thesis has played out.

Bankroll Management

Keep 20‚Äì30% of your portfolio in cash at all times. This lets you capitalize on sudden opportunities (news events, market dislocations) without being forced to sell existing positions at a loss.

Emotional Discipline

Set entry and exit prices before placing a trade. If a position drops 30% from entry, re-evaluate your thesis objectively. If the thesis is broken, cut losses. If it still holds, consider averaging down only if you have conviction.

Edge Identification

Your edge comes from information asymmetry or better probability estimation. If you can't articulate why the market is mispriced, you don't have an edge. Never trade just because a price "feels" wrong.
