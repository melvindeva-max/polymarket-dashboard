Position Sizing Rules

Follow the Kelly Criterion lite approach — never bet more than 2× your edge as a percentage of bankroll:

  • High confidence (85%+): Up to 20% of available cash per position. These are near-locks with short resolution times.
  • Medium confidence (60–84%): 10–15% per position. Core holdings that drive portfolio returns.
  • Speculative (below 60%): 5–10% max. Asymmetric payoffs — small bets, big potential wins.
  • Never exceed 25% of your total account value in a single market.

Risk Management

  • Diversify across categories: Don't put more than 40% in any one category (sports, politics, etc.).
  • Time diversify: Mix short-term (weeks), medium-term (1–3 months), and long-term (6+ months) positions.
  • Set exit rules: If a position drops 50% from entry, reassess your thesis. If the thesis is broken, cut losses.
  • Lock in profits: If a position reaches 90¢+, consider selling 50% to lock in gains rather than riding to resolution.
  • Correlation awareness: Avoid stacking positions that all depend on the same outcome (e.g., multiple Trump-related bets).
  • Liquidity matters: Prefer markets with $100K+ liquidity. Thin markets have wide spreads that eat into your edge.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Anchoring to purchase price: Your entry price doesn't affect the market. Evaluate positions based on current odds vs. your assessment.
  • Overconfidence on favorites: Buying at 90¢+ has terrible risk/reward unless you're 95%+ confident. A 90¢ position risks $9 to make $1.
  • Ignoring resolution criteria: Always read the fine print on how a market resolves. Ambiguous resolution = hidden risk.
  • Chasing volume: High volume doesn't mean good opportunity. It often means the market is efficiently priced.
