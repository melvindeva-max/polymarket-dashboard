Strategy Guide

Position Sizing Rules

For a ~$80 portfolio, follow these guidelines to manage risk effectively:

  • Max single position: 20% of total portfolio ($16). Never risk more than this on any single market.
  • High-confidence bets (85%+ odds): Allocate 40-50% of available capital. These are your bread and butter — low margin but high win rate.
  • Medium-confidence bets (60-84%): Allocate 35-40% of available capital. Better returns per share but higher variance.
  • Speculative longshots (<40%): Cap at 15% of available capital. These are lottery tickets — size accordingly.
  • Always keep 10-15% cash reserve for opportunistic plays when markets move quickly.

Risk Management

  • Diversify across categories: Don't put all eggs in sports or politics. Aim for 3-4 categories minimum.
  • Time diversify: Mix quick-resolution markets (days/weeks) with longer-term plays (months). Quick markets recycle capital faster.
  • Correlation risk: Avoid multiple bets that fail together (e.g., all sports bets losing if there's a scandal/lockout).
  • Exit strategy: If a position moves against you by 20%+, reassess the thesis. Don't hold out of stubbornness.
  • Take profits: If a 50¢ bet moves to 85¢+, consider selling to lock in gains rather than waiting for full resolution.

Finding Edge

The key to profitable prediction market trading is finding situations where you know something the market doesn't, or where the market is slow to update:

  • News catalysts: When breaking news shifts probabilities, markets often lag by minutes or hours. Be fast.
  • Domain expertise: If you follow a sport/industry closely, you may spot mispricing before casual traders do.
  • Resolution criteria: Always read the fine print. Sometimes markets resolve differently than the title implies.
  • Liquidity gaps: Markets with less liquidity tend to be more mispriced. The tradeoff is harder exits.
