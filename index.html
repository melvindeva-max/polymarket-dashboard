🧠 Strategy Guide

Position Sizing Rules

Follow the Kelly Criterion lite approach for prediction markets:

  • High confidence (85%+ implied, Risk 1): Allocate up to 45% of available capital. These are near-certainties where you're collecting small, reliable returns.
  • Medium confidence (60-84%, Risk 2): Allocate ~40%. The bread and butter ‚Äî look for markets where your estimate diverges from the price by 5%+ points.
  • Speculative (Risk 3): Cap at 15%. Asymmetric bets with 5x+ payoff potential. Accept total loss as likely.

Risk Management

  • Never risk more than 25% of total portfolio on a single market.
  • Diversify across categories: Don't concentrate in one area (you're already heavy on sports ‚Äî balance with politics/finance/tech).
  • Set exit rules: If a position moves against you by 15%+, reassess the thesis before adding.
  • Lock in profits: When a position is up 50%+, consider selling half to de-risk.
  • Time decay matters: Markets approaching resolution date with high prices are safer. Low-price longshots decay to zero if the event doesn't happen.
  • Liquidity check: Avoid markets with <$10K liquidity ‚Äî slippage will eat your edge.

When to Exit a Position

  • Your thesis has been invalidated by new information
  • The market has moved to reflect your estimate (edge gone)
  • Better opportunities appear that require rebalancing
  • You need the capital for higher-EV plays
