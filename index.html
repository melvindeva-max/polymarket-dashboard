Strategy Guide

Position Sizing Rules

Kelly Criterion (Simplified): Bet size = (edge / odds). For a market at 60¬¢ where you believe true probability is 70%, your edge is 10% and optimal Kelly fraction is ~16.7% of bankroll. Use half-Kelly for safety.

Max Position Sizes:

‚Ä¢ Risk 1 (85%+ confidence): Up to 25% of available capital per position

‚Ä¢ Risk 2 (60-84%): Up to 15% per position

‚Ä¢ Risk 3 (speculative): Max 10% per position, cap total speculative at 15% of portfolio

Portfolio Targets:

‚Ä¢ 45% high-confidence, 40% medium, 15% speculative

‚Ä¢ Diversify across 3+ categories minimum

Risk Management Tips

1. Time Decay Awareness: Markets closer to resolution have less uncertainty. A 90¬¢ YES with 1 week left is very different from 90¬¢ with 6 months left. Prefer shorter resolution windows for high-confidence bets.

2. Liquidity Check: Always check liquidity before entering. Low-liquidity markets (<$1K) will eat your returns in slippage. Target markets with $10K+ liquidity for positions over $5.

3. Exit Strategy: Set mental stop-losses. If a position drops 30%+ from entry, reassess your thesis. Don't hold losers hoping for recovery without new information.

4. Correlation Risk: Your portfolio is heavy on sports. Multiple sports bets can all lose in the same bad week. Diversify across uncorrelated categories (politics, crypto, AI, culture).

5. Bankroll Protection: Never risk more than you can afford to lose. Keep at least 20% of your total portfolio in cash or near-certain positions as a buffer.

Key Terms

EV (Expected Value): Your expected profit per dollar bet. EV = (your probability √ó payout) - bet. Positive EV = good bet over time.

Edge: The difference between your estimated probability and the market price. Edge = your prob - market price.

Implied Probability: The market price IS the implied probability. A 60¬¢ YES price means the market thinks there's a 60% chance of YES.

Resolution: When a market officially settles. YES shares pay $1, NO shares pay $0. Unresolved shares can be traded at market price.

Slippage: The price difference between your expected trade and actual execution, worse in low-liquidity markets.
