🎯 Position Sizing Rules

  • Kelly Criterion (Simplified): Never bet more than (edge / odds). If you estimate 60% probability on a 50¬¢ market, max bet = (0.60 - 0.50) / 0.50 = 20% of bankroll.
  • Max single position: 30% of available capital for high-confidence plays, 15% for medium, 5-10% for speculative.
  • Portfolio allocation: ~45% high-confidence (85%+ odds), ~40% medium (60-84%), ~15% speculative longshots.
  • Diversify categories: Don't overload on one sector. Mix politics, crypto, finance, sports, tech.

📈 Expected Value (EV) Calculator

EV = (Probability of Win × Payout) - (Probability of Loss × Stake)




🛡️ Risk Management

  • Stop losses: If a position drops 40%+ from entry, reassess thesis. Don't hold on hope.
  • Take profits: At 90%+ prices, consider selling ‚Äî remaining upside is small vs. risk of resolution failure.
  • Correlation risk: Multiple sports bets resolve similarly. Tariff/recession bets may correlate. Balance accordingly.
  • Liquidity check: Markets under $10K liquidity = wide spreads. You may not get the listed price.
  • Time decay: High-confidence markets near resolution (>90¬¢) have limited upside. Favor markets with 2-8 weeks to resolution.
  • Never all-in: Keep 10-20% cash reserve for sudden opportunities or averaging down.

💡 Pro Tips

  • News catalyst trading: Buy before expected catalysts (earnings, court rulings, events). Markets often misprice timing.
  • Contrarian value: When a market moves 10%+ on emotion (not facts), fade the move. Overreactions create value.
  • Multi-market arbitrage: Check if related markets are mispriced relative to each other (e.g., "recession" vs. "unemployment >5%").
  • Resolution clarity: Always read resolution criteria. Ambiguous markets = higher risk of disputed outcomes.
