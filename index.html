Position Sizing Rules

Kelly Criterion (Simplified): Bet size = (edge / odds). For a market at 60¢ where you believe true probability is 75%, your edge = 0.15. Kelly says bet ~37.5% of bankroll. In practice, use half-Kelly (18-19%) to reduce volatility.

Portfolio Allocation Guide:
• ~45% in high-confidence (Risk 1) — Markets at 85%+ where resolution is near-certain. Target: 3-5% return on capital.
• ~40% in medium-confidence (Risk 2) — Markets at 60-84% with clear informational edge. Target: 20-50% return.
• ~15% in speculative (Risk 3) — Longshots under 40¢ with asymmetric upside. Accept frequent losses for occasional 3-10x winners.

Max single position: Never more than 30% of bankroll in one market. Current largest position (Barcelona La Liga) is 31% — slightly over, consider trimming.

Risk Management Tips

1. Diversify across categories. Your current portfolio is sports-heavy. Mix in politics, crypto, AI, and culture markets to reduce correlation risk.

2. Set stop-losses mentally. If a position drops 50% from entry, reassess — has the thesis changed? If yes, sell. Don't hold losers out of hope.

3. Lock in profits on winners. When a position goes from 60¢ to 90¢, consider selling half. The last 10¢ of upside has the worst risk/reward.

4. Watch resolution dates. Markets close to resolution at extreme prices (95%+) are usually correctly priced. Your edge is in markets 2-8 weeks from resolution where information is still being priced in.

5. Beware thin liquidity. If a market has under $2K liquidity, your order will move the price. Stick to markets with $5K+ liquidity for positions over $3.

6. Track your actual hit rate. After 20+ trades, calculate: (wins / total trades) and (avg win / avg loss). You need (hit rate × avg win) > ((1 - hit rate) × avg loss) to be profitable long-term.
