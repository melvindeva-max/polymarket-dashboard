üìê Position Sizing Rules

  • High confidence (85%+, Risk 1): Up to 45% of available capital. These are near-certainties. Small upside but reliable.
  • Medium confidence (60-84%, Risk 2): 35-40% of available capital. Good EV, moderate variance.
  • Speculative (below 60%, Risk 3): 15-20% max. Lottery-style bets. Size for max loss you can stomach.
  • Never put more than 30% in a single market regardless of confidence level.
  • Prefer markets resolving in 1-3 months for faster capital recycling and compounding.

üßÆ Expected Value Formula

EV = (P_win √ó profit) ‚àí (P_lose √ó stake)

Where: P_win = your assessed probability, profit = (stake / price) ‚àí stake

Only bet when EV > 0 AND the market price is lower than your true confidence. The bigger the gap, the better the edge.

Example: Market at 70¬¢, you think 80% chance ‚Üí Edge = 10%, EV = 0.80 √ó (1/0.70 ‚àí 1) √ó stake ‚àí 0.20 √ó stake

‚öñÔ∏è Risk Management

  • Diversify categories: Don't hold more than 50% in any single category (sports, politics, crypto, etc.)
  • Time diversification: Mix short-term (weeks) and medium-term (months) positions.
  • Avoid long-duration lock-up: Markets resolving 2+ years out tie up capital with enormous uncertainty.
  • Have an exit strategy: If a position moves strongly in your favor, consider taking partial profits.
  • Don't chase losses: If a market moves against you, reassess the thesis ‚Äî don't double down blindly.

üîç Finding Edge

  • Information asymmetry: Do you know something the market doesn't? (e.g., insider sports knowledge, domain expertise)
  • Recency bias exploitation: Markets often overweight recent news and underweight base rates.
  • Cross-market arbitrage: Compare related markets (e.g., April AI vs June AI pricing gaps).
  • Liquidity premium: Illiquid markets may be mispriced due to lack of informed traders.
  • Resolution criteria: Always read the exact resolution criteria ‚Äî markets often resolve differently than you expect.
