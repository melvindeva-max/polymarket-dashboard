Position Sizing Rules

Proper position sizing is critical to long-term success. Use these principles:

  • Kelly Criterion: f* = (EV / payout) where f* is the fraction of bankroll to risk. For positive EV bets, this maximizes long-term growth.
  • Maximum per trade: Never risk more than 20% of your portfolio on a single trade, regardless of confidence level.
  • Bankroll allocation: Divide total capital into tiers: 45% safe (96¬¢+), 40% medium (30-80¬¢), 15% speculative (under 30¬¢).
  • Risk scaling: Higher confidence should mean larger positions, but only up to the 20% max. A 95% confidence at 1% risk might be 15% of bankroll, while 50% confidence at 2% risk should be 5% max.
  • Diversification: Spread across multiple markets and categories. Don't concentrate in politics or sports alone.
Expected Value (EV) Calculator

Expected value determines whether a bet is worth taking mathematically:

EV = (Win Probability √ó Win Amount) - (Loss Probability √ó Loss Amount)

Example: A bet at 60¬¢ with $10 allocation:

  • Win case: 60% probability √ó $16.67 payout = $10.00
  • Loss case: 40% probability √ó $10 loss = -$4.00
  • EV = $10.00 - $4.00 = +$6.00 (positive EV, take the bet)

Only take bets with positive EV. A +$0.50 EV on $10 risk yields +5% returns.

Risk Management Framework

Categorize all markets by risk level:

Risk 1 (Safe) 90¬¢+ pricing, near-resolution dates, clear outcomes, institutional backing
Risk 2 (Medium) 30-89¬¢ pricing, 1-6 month timelines, defined events, some uncertainty
Risk 3 (High) Under 30¬¢ pricing, 6+ month timelines, ambiguous criteria, binary outcomes
Portfolio Allocation Targets

Maintain balanced exposure across risk tiers:

  • 45% Safe (Risk 1): Capital preservation. Fed chair, World Cup favorites, near-lock scenarios. Target 2-5% EV return on this bucket.
  • 40% Medium (Risk 2): Growth. Balanced risk/reward bets with 1-2 year horizons. Target 15-25% EV return on this bucket.
  • 15% Speculative (Risk 3): Lottery tickets. Low probabilities with high payoffs. Accept 0-50% EV loss for potential 10x+ upside.

Rebalance quarterly. If safe positions underperform, reallocate from medium/speculative tiers back to safety.

Thesis Development Checklist

Before placing any trade, validate your thesis against these criteria:

  • Do I have clear data supporting my probability estimate?
  • What would need to change for me to be wrong?
  • Is the market price inefficient relative to fair value?
  • Can I articulate this thesis in 2-3 sentences?
  • What is my exit strategy (profit target, stop loss)?
  • Have I accounted for base rates and reference class outcomes?
  • Is this a conviction bet or just speculation?
Timing and Market Dynamics

Market prices shift based on information flow, sentiment, and time decay:

  • Early entry: Take positions 3-6 months before resolution when consensus hasn't crystallized.
  • News reaction: Markets often overreact to positive/negative developments. Look for mean-reversion opportunities within 24-48 hours.
  • Time decay: As resolution approaches, prices become binary. Exit before the final week unless you have near-certain conviction.
  • Volume spikes: High volume periods (30-50x normal) indicate retail interest. Institutional flows may offer better pricing.
  • Closing dates: Markets become illiquid 7 days before close. Avoid building new positions in final week.
Avoiding Common Pitfalls
  • Overconfidence: Your 85% conviction is often someone else's 50%. Account for model uncertainty.
  • Sunk cost fallacy: Exit losing positions if your thesis changes, even if you're underwater.
  • Availability bias: Don't bet on recent news excessively. Check if market has already priced it in.
  • Groupthink: Markets trend popular narratives. Find contrarian value where consensus is wrong.
  • Leverage: Never borrow to trade prediction markets. The binary nature makes leverage extremely dangerous.
  • Illiquid positions: Avoid markets with under $1M volume. You can't exit efficiently.
Performance Tracking

Measure your actual results quarterly:

  • ROI: (Gains / Capital Deployed) √ó 100
  • Win rate: Winning bets / Total bets closed
  • Sharpe ratio: Average return / Standard deviation of returns
  • Calibration: For bets you marked 70% confidence, did ~70% actually resolve YES?
  • Thesis accuracy: Were your reasons for taking the bet actually the drivers of outcome?

Track these metrics in a spreadsheet. They reveal your edges and blind spots.
